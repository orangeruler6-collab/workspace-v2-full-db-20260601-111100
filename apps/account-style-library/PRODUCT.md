# Account Style Library Workbench

## Users

This is a local desktop workbench for creators and operators who collect high-performing Bilibili and Douyin content, maintain transcripts, distill account or project style cards, and generate copy from those references.

Users are focused, task-oriented, and often moving between collection, transcription, style editing, drafting, and asset generation in one sitting. They need clear state, dense information, and safe local-file behavior more than marketing flourish.

## Product Purpose

The product turns local files in `style-library` into a usable content operations system. It helps users:

- Collect account videos and source materials.
- Generate or edit transcripts and style cards.
- Combine accounts into project styles.
- Draft copy and derivative assets.
- Publish selected output to Feishu when configured.

## Tone

Chinese UX copy should feel like a capable local tool: concise, specific, calm, and actionable. Avoid marketing language, vague success messages, or raw low-level errors when a next step can be named.

## Strategic Principles

- Protect local assets first. Deletion, migration, and batch rewriting must be conservative.
- Keep the desktop experience dense and scannable.
- Use motion to explain state changes, not to decorate the interface.
- Make fallback behavior visible and non-alarming.
- Prefer explicit progress, selection, and save states over passive text.

## Anti-References

- Marketing landing-page composition.
- Dark neon dashboards.
- Glassmorphism as a default surface treatment.
- Large decorative hero sections.
- Over-simplified empty screens that hide the next useful action.
